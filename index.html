3. 5 Whys Technique

The 5 Whys technique is used to identify the root cause of the failure mode:

  1. Why are students unable to register for classes?
    Because the registration system crashes during peak times.
  2. Why does the system crash during peak times?
    Because the server cannot handle the high volume of requests.
  3. Why can’t the server handle the high volume of requests?
    Because it lacks sufficient resources (e.g., memory, processing power).
  4. Why does it lack sufficient resources?
    Because the university did not anticipate the increase in student enrollment.
  5. Why wasn’t the increase in enrollment anticipated?
    Because there was no mechanism to track and predict enrollment trends.

Root Cause: Lack of predictive analytics and scalable infrastructure to handle enrollment growth.

Solution: Invest in scalable server infrastructure and implement predictive analytics for enrollment trends.

4. Comparison of FMEA and 5 Whys

Below is a comparison of the two techniques:

Aspect FMEA 5 Whys
Purpose Identify and prioritize potential failure modes in a system. Drill down to the root cause of a specific problem.
Complexity More comprehensive and structured. Simple and quick to apply.
Scope Best for systems with multiple components and interactions. Best for problems with a clear cause-and-effect relationship.
Output List of failure modes, their causes, effects, and suggested improvements. Single root cause and a proposed solution.
Applicability Preventive measures for complex systems. Reactive problem-solving for specific issues.
